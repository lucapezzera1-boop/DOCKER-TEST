What is Super Smash Bros. Melee?

Super Smash Bros. Melee is a fighting game developed by Nintendo for the GameCube, released in 2001 in Japan and the United States, and in 2002 in Europe.

The game is known for its incredibly tactical gameplay, which requires skill and strategy to dominate the battlefield.

My favorite character is Marth: he is one of the best characters in the game, known for his ability in ranged combat thanks to his long sword and deadly spacing.

Super Smash Bros. Melee (SSBM) is also known for having many bugs, which are essential for high-level competitive play.

Some of the most famous bugs include:
  • Wavedash: allows lateral movement without losing speed, increasing the character's mobility (especially Luigi).
  • L-canceling: reduces the recovery time after an aerial attack, allowing for faster follow-up actions.
  • Port Priority: a bug that provides a slight advantage based on the port where the GameCube controller is inserted. Players in the higher port have barely visible advantages.

I often play SSBM with friends every evening, and I'm reasonably good at it, modestly speaking, and I have a channel on YouTube where I share gaming moments.

What is Crobat?

Crobat is a Pokémon introduced in the second generation (Gold and Silver for Game Boy). It evolves from Golbat when it reaches a certain level of friendship.

"Its fangs are so sharp, if it bites you in the dark and sucks your blood, you won't notice any pain or realize you've been bitten." - Pokémon Ultra Sun

"It feeds on the blood of living people and Pokémon. If it can't drink any blood for even a short while, it becomes weak and unable to fly." - Pokémon Ultra Moon

My adventures with Super Smash Bros. Melee

My Introduction to Melee

I started playing Super Smash Bros. Melee in late March 2024, on my dad's laptop, which he gave me when he upgraded. I used this setup for school and for fun. To play Melee, I used a Nintendo Switch controller, connected via USB.

I didn't play Melee much, as it was difficult and the other people I played with were experienced, while I was 13. As a result, I didn't play much in the summer, or in my free time.

I continued playing Melee after a long time towards the end of the year, when I changed my setup for personal use, but then I stopped playing for a while.

but in March 4th, 2025 everything changed.

That day, a stranger invited me to play Melee, and I declined. But he persisted and convinced me. After playing a few games, I discovered we were the same age and skill level. He also lived in Tunisia, so we had the same time slot (ideal for organizing matches).

Since then, we've seen each other daily playing SSBM and other games. We're still in touch, and we both upload Smash Bros Melee videos to our YouTube channels. mine, My friend's
